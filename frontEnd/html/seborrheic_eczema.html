What is seborrheic egzma?

Seborrheic dermatitis is a common skin condition that mainly affects your scalp. It causes scaly patches, red skin and stubborn dandruff. Seborrheic dermatitis can also affect oily areas of the body, such as the face, sides of the nose, eyebrows, ears, eyelids and chest.

Symptoms

  • Skin flakes (dandruff) on your scalp, hair, eybrows, beard or mustache
  • Patches of greasy skin covered with flaky white or yellow scales or crust on the scalp, face, sides of the nose, eyebrows, ears, eyelids, chest, armpits, groin area or under the breasts
  • Red skin
  • Itching

When to see a doctor

See your doctor if you're so uncomfortable that you are losing sleep or being distracted form your daily routines, your condition is causing embarrassment and anxiety, you suspect your skin is infected, or you've tried self-care steps without success

How to treat seborrheic eczema

  • Creams, shampoos or ointments that control inflammation
  • Antifungal gels
  • Antifungal medication
