What is cellulitis?

Cellulitis is a common, potentially serious bacterial skin infection. The affected skin appears swollen and red and is typically painful and warm to the touch.

Symptoms

  • Red area of skin that tends to expand
  • Swelling
  • Tenderness
  • Pain
  • Warmth
  • Fever

When to see a doctor

It's important to identify and treat cellulitis early because the condition can spread rapidly throughout your body. Seek emergency care if: You have a red, swollen, tender rash or a rash that's changing rapidly, or if you have a fever. See your doctor, preferably that day, if: You have a rash that's red, swollen, tender and warm — and it's expanding — but without fever

How to treat cellulitis

  • Cellulitis treatment usually includes a prescription oral antibiotic.
